Why should US Steel Suppliers and Distributors turn to Technology to improve efficiency and create value?

The steel industry has been thriving for the last century, especially in the United States. So far in 2020, the domestic raw steel production was 1,383,000 net tons. To compete in the global scale, steel companies are adding value to their supply chain through digital transformation.

Focusing on Value

The industry has remained predominantly offline through the ages. Even as information technology and automation swept across the world, the steel industry has retained its fairly conservative business model.

Due to the increased competition, the industry is reconsidering this decades old strategy. They are looking to improve efficiency, reduce procurement costs and provide value to the end customer through superior quality and design. 

Combating the costs of procurement

Several forecasts postulate that the increasing price of raw materials like iron ore, coils, scrap among others will increase the manufacturing costs of suppliers. This in turn, will increase the cost price of steel, as suppliers strive to maintain their profit margins.

 By creating cost control techniques and operational efficiency, they might be able to maintain a steady profit margin. One of the primary ways to achieve that is by utilizing technology and digital transformation. 

By achieving efficiency in operations, these suppliers will be able to provide competitive pricing for the products, without compromising their profit margins.
